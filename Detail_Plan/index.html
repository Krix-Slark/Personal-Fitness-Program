Bench Press

\

The bench press is the gold standard of upper body strength exercises, targeting the chest, shoulders, and triceps in one powerful compound movement. This fundamental exercise builds pressing power, muscular endurance, and upper body mass when performed with proper technique and progressive overload.

Sets x Reps

3 x 12

Intensity

High

Heart Rate

70-80% max HR

Shoulder Press

The shoulder press is a fundamental exercise for building upper body strength, targeting the deltoids, triceps, and upper chest. This compound movement enhances shoulder stability, promotes muscle growth, and improves overall pressing power.

Sets x Reps

3 x 12

Intensity

Medium

Heart Rate

70-80% max HR

Lat Pulldown

The lat pulldown is an essential exercise for developing upper body strength, particularly targeting the latissimus dorsi muscles in the back. This movement helps improve posture, enhance pulling strength, and build a V-shaped physique.

Sets x Reps

3 x 12

Intensity

Medium

Heart Rate

70-80% max HR

Squat

The squat is a fundamental exercise for building lower body strength, targeting the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. This compound movement enhances overall leg power, stability, and mobility.

Sets x Reps

3 x 12

Intensity

High

Heart Rate

70-85% max HR

Plank

The plank is a core strengthening exercise that targets the abdominal muscles, lower back, and shoulders. It helps improve stability, posture, and overall core strength.

Sets x Reps

2 x 30 sec

Intensity

Low

Heart Rate

60-70% max HR

Battle Rope

Battle rope exercises are a dynamic way to build strength and endurance. They engage multiple muscle groups, improve cardiovascular fitness, and enhance overall athletic performance.

Sets x Reps

Intensity

High

Heart Rate

70-90% max HR

Treadmill

The treadmill is a popular cardio machine that simulates walking or running indoors. It provides a convenient way to improve cardiovascular fitness, burn calories, and enhance lower body strength.

Time

10 mins

Intensity

Low

Heart Rate

60-70% max HR

Extra Tips

1. Warm-Up and Cool Down: Always start with a 5-10 minute warm-up to prepare your muscles and end with a cool-down to aid recovery.

2. Proper Form: Focus on maintaining proper form to prevent injuries and maximize effectiveness.

3. Hydration: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout.

4. Rest: Ensure you get adequate rest between sets and exercises to allow muscle recovery.

5. Nutrition: Fuel your body with a balanced diet rich in protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats to support your fitness goals.
